# 1. THE EQUATIONS OF MOTION - Classical Mechanics (Landau & Lifshitz)

This folder contains exhaustive solutions and high-fidelity numerical simulations for Chapter 1 of Landau and Lifshitz's *Course of Theoretical Physics, Vol. 1*.

## 📄 Contents
*   **[V.1_Classical_Mechanics_Landau_Sol1_The_Equations_of_Motion.pdf](./V.1_Classical_Mechanics_Landau_Sol1_The_Equations_of_Motion.pdf):** 40+ pages of detailed LaTeX solutions. Includes step-by-step derivations of Lagrangians, Hamiltonians, and analytical treatments for complex pendulums.
*   **[Braket_Landau_1_Simulations.ipynb](./Braket_Landau_1_Simulations.ipynb):** Python notebook (Google Colab) with advanced integrators for non-linear systems, phase space maps, and energy conservation benchmarks.

## 🎯 Key Concepts & Deep Dives
- **Problem 1.1 - Double Pendulum Dynamics:** Derivation of coupled non-linear equations and transition to chaos via Poincaré maps.
- **Problem 1.2 - Movable Support Systems:** Horizontal recoil analysis and numerical verification of Noether's Theorem (conservation of $p_x$).
- **Problem 1.3(a,b,c) - The Kapitza Effect:** Dynamic stabilization of the inverted pendulum through vertical high-frequency modulation.
- **Problem 1.4 - Watt's Centrifugal Governor:** 
    - **3D Simulations:** Visualization of the rotating flyballs and their equilibrium manifolds.
    - **Formalism Comparison:** A detailed "Head-to-Head" between the **Routhian** (for cyclic coordinates) vs. **Hamiltonian** vs. **Lagrangian** approaches to find the critical frequency.
- **Parametric Resonance:** Stability analysis of driven harmonic oscillators and Mathieu equation transitions.
- **Phase Space Topology:** Identification of KAM tori, chaotic seas, and Lyapunov exponents.
